2. What Data We Collect

Account & Identity Data

When you sign in, we collect:

  • Your email address (via Clerk, our authentication provider)
  • Your first and last name (if provided during sign-in)

Analytics Data

We use PostHog to understand how the app is used. We collect:

  • Your name and email address (to identify active users in our internal dashboard)
  • A stable user identifier (your Clerk account ID)
  • App usage events (e.g. overlays shown, dance completions, onboarding steps)
  • The names (package identifiers) of the apps you choose to monitor and how many minutes you use them against your daily allowance — this is core to the app's function and is only collected for apps you select
  • Crash logs and diagnostics (error messages, stack traces, and a short trail of recent in-app actions) to help us fix bugs
  • App version and platform (Android/iOS)
  • A stable device identifier (randomly generated, not linked to hardware)

We do not collect precise location, contacts, photos, or financial information.

Accessibility Permission

The Android Accessibility permission is used only to detect which app is in the foreground so we can show the dance break overlay. We only read package names — we do not capture content shown inside other apps.

Advertising Data

We use Google AdMob to display ads. AdMob may collect advertising ID and approximate location (country/region). See Google's privacy policy for details.

6. Your Rights

Under GDPR (EU/EEA) and similar laws, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of your personal data
  • Correction — ask us to fix inaccurate data
  • Deletion — ask us to delete your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restriction — ask us to stop processing your data
  • Objection — object to any processing
  • Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format

Email dev.prasanna.kadam@gmail.com to exercise any right. We will respond within 30 days. For analytics data, we can delete your PostHog person profile on request. To delete your account and all associated data, see Delete Your Account.

If you are in the EU and believe we have not handled your data correctly, you may lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.
